# Draft Tesserac Terms of Service

This is a draft operating policy for an in-game ComputerCraft service. It is not legal advice. Review and edit it before presenting it to players.

## 1. Services Covered

These terms cover use of Tesserac services, including:

- TesseracID accounts
- HyperCube phones
- HyperNet messaging
- Tesserac web pages
- Bank of Ba$h accounts and ATM access
- App store packages
- Moderation and reporting tools

## 2. Account Requirements

Users must provide accurate account information when creating or using Tesserac services.

For Bank of Ba$h accounts, users must provide their correct Minecraft name. Accounts using false, misleading, or incorrect names may be closed by Tesserac administrators.

Users are responsible for activity performed through their TesseracID, phone, and registered devices.

## 3. Acceptable Use

Users agree not to use Tesserac services to:

- Harass, threaten, or abuse other players.
- Send harmful, hateful, explicit, or disruptive messages.
- Impersonate another player, business, moderator, or Tesserac service.
- Attempt to bypass account, phone, banking, web, or device restrictions.
- Exploit bugs, duplicate currency, fake deposits, or manipulate ATM flows.
- Publish web pages or app content that disrupts the server or targets other users.
- Attempt unauthorized access to another user's files, messages, bank account, phone number, or TesseracID.

## 4. Messaging and Reports

Phone messages may be reported by recipients or other users with access to the relevant conversation.

Reported messages may be reviewed by authorized moderators. Moderators may use account lookup tools to connect reports to Tesserac usernames, phone numbers, Tesserac IDs, Minecraft names, and related bank account records.

## 5. Banking

Bank of Ba$h balances are in-game service balances, not real money.

Users must only deposit valid accepted in-game currency through trusted Bank of Ba$h branches or ATMs.

Withdrawals, deposits, ATM fees, transfers, phone bills, and other service charges may be recorded in account history.

Tesserac administrators may close bank accounts that:

- Use incorrect or misleading Minecraft names.
- Are used for fraud, exploitation, or abuse.
- Are connected to bypassing trusted ATM or branch rules.
- Are otherwise unsafe for continued service access.

Account closure may preserve an archived record including final balance, username, Minecraft name, closure reason, and closure time.

## 6. Phone Service Billing

A phone number requires an open Bank of Ba$h account linked to the caller's TesseracID.

The first phone subscription week is free. Future renewals may charge the linked bank account weekly.

If the linked account is missing or has insufficient funds, phone service may be limited until payment succeeds.

## 7. Web Pages and Apps

Users are responsible for pages and apps they publish or install.

Tesserac administrators may remove, block, or restrict web pages, app packages, domains, or origin servers that are harmful, abusive, misleading, or technically disruptive.

Routed web origins and trusted devices must not be used to impersonate official Tesserac services.

## 8. Moderation

Authorized moderators may review reports, look up account information, authorize other moderators, and take account actions allowed by the moderation portal.

The official `tesserac` account may perform elevated actions such as closing bank accounts for incorrect signup names.

Moderation decisions may consider message content, report history, account metadata, phone numbers, Minecraft names, bank records, and device registration history.

## 9. Data Stored

Tesserac services may store:

- Tesserac username and Tesserac ID
- Display name
- Minecraft name for bank accounts
- Registered device IDs and scopes
- Phone number, phone subscription state, and message records
- Bank account balance, transaction history, ATM activity, and closure records
- Web domains, published pages, and origin registration data
- Moderation reports and authorized moderator records

## 10. Security

Users must not share passwords, session tokens, admin tokens, trusted device identities, or disk contents that could allow unauthorized access.

Users must not intentionally run modified phone ROMs to bypass service checks. The server may reject phones that do not match the approved ROM checksum.

## 11. Service Changes

Tesserac administrators may update, restart, restrict, or disable services to fix bugs, protect users, prevent abuse, or maintain the server.

Service data may be repaired, migrated, archived, or removed when necessary for maintenance or moderation.

## 12. Enforcement

Violations may result in:

- Warning
- Message or report review
- Phone service restriction
- Web domain or app removal
- Bank account closure
- Device trust revocation
- TesseracID restriction
- Referral to server staff for additional action

## 13. Appeals

Users may contact server staff or Tesserac administrators to ask about moderation actions. Administrators may restore access or correct records if an action was made in error.

## 14. Draft Notice

This document is a starting point. Before public use, replace this section with the server's preferred contact method, staff names or roles, appeal process, and final effective date.
